About Me

Hello, and welcome to my blog! My name is Fineas and I'm a Computer Science Major at Skidmore College. I'm interested in financial systems and money. I first started out investing because I was looking to get rich quick, but through the few years I've been actively involved in the finance world, I have seen how many people are exploited or suppressed through complex financial structures. Educating people and providing services that give a better understanding of markets, lending, and other financial products is now something that I'm interested in pursuing as a career.

Hobbies

I run a cryptocurrency YouTube channel where I talk about exciting new projects in the crypto space. I'm also big into NFTs. I own a few monkey pictures, in the hopes that one day they will be worth millions. If you having even a fleeting interest in either of these areas please ask me about them! I would love to share some of what I've learned.

Writing Content

I'll be posting the required assignments for the Mapping Rome course on this blog. I would like to include things about economics or finance in my writing when I have the chance, but I'm also fascinated by history, and I imagine that much of my work may be devoid of financial topics.

I love fashion, and the thought of Italian shirts and shoes has me drooling; I can't wait to get a taste of the best men's garments in the world. I'm sure there will be entire blog analyses on the refined Italian goods that I inspect, as well.

I do want this to be a resource I can point family and friends towards when they ask about my experience in the class and the city. I hope that this blog may be able to capture, in some small part, my over-joyous excitement for my first venture outside of the United States.

Having a full course dedicated to the city, and learning from fantastic professors with expertise in all aspects of Rome, adds a layer of knowledge to the travel experience that I've never had before. I look forward to analyzing every bend and cobble of the Roman roads, and getting a feel for one of the most powerful cities of the ancient world.
