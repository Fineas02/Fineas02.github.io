Four Senses Rome

Fineas Jackson

Jun 13, 2023 • 1 min read

The leaves seem to sing in bliss, full of gratitude toward the cool breeze. As I sit, a hushed melody of summer fills my ears. Feet pound on the gravel nearby, each step punctuated by a resounding crunch.

A cry echoes from a child, followed by the thwack of a foot connecting with a ball. A deeper voice shouts “Bravo, bravo,” and I hear the clapping of hands. From the same direction, I’m bombarded by the rich smell of cooking meat.

And the breeze flows again, as if to reward me for a hard day of travel. The whispering leaves seem to encourage me to keep pushing forward. “But take a quick rest now,” they say. “We’re here to take care of you.”

A smile forms on my lips and my shoulders relax. Suddenly, there’s a loud exclamation right in front of me, and I tense back up. A young voice exclaims in a breathy tone, “Catch me if you can,” followed by fast footfalls on gravel. A few seconds pass and then I feel a rush of air as someone else hustles past me.

All is calm for a moment, and then I take it all in. The way the sun kisses my face, persistent, but not overbearing. The scent of the air, fresh and clean. I’m so grateful in this moment. To be here in Rome. To live the present moment among the prolific monuments of the past. It’s the perfect end to the day, and the perfect way to start the summer.
